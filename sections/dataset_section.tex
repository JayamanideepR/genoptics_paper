\section{Dataset}

We construct the released lens-assembly design datasets in three stages: collection and processing of a broad patent-derived corpus of 2,571 designs; standardization and shortlisting of 1,253 of those designs; and generation of 1,125,080 synthetic designs through controlled perturbation and physics-based post-processing of the shortlisted designs. The patent-derived designs provide realistic reference families across surface counts, optical configurations, aperture locations, field angles, and material choices, while the synthetic expansion broadens coverage around those families. The remainder of this section describes these stages in greater detail. Subsection~\ref{sec:dataset_patent_reference} introduces the full patent-derived corpus and its linked CodeV and tabular representations. Subsection~\ref{sec:dataset_patent_shortlisting} describes the standardization and shortlisting process used to obtain the patent subset for synthetic expansion and generative modeling. Subsection~\ref{sec:dataset_synthetic_expansion} then presents the synthetic expansion workflow and resulting expanded dataset.

\subsection{Patent-Derived Reference Designs}
\label{sec:dataset_patent_reference}

The patent-derived reference corpus contains 2,571 optical system designs assembled from three sources: the CodeV patent library~\cite{codev}, which primarily contains Japanese and Russian patents; the Zemax Zebase library~\cite{zemax_opticstudio}, which predominantly contains United States patents; and a public lens assembly design file exchange containing community-submitted designs from diverse sources~\cite{reiley_lens_design_exchange}. Each released design retains the best available source attribution, such as a patent identifier, textbook or journal citation, or other source record.

All collected designs undergo a common CodeV preparation and evaluation workflow. Source artifacts are converted to CodeV's sequence-file format (\texttt{.seq}) where needed. When a source file contains generic glass codes or obsolete material labels rather than materials from available optical catalogs, the CodeV \texttt{GLASSFIT} procedure is used to update its material assignments. The image-plane location is then re-optimized to account for any resulting prescription changes. Dependent optical quantities and performance metrics are subsequently evaluated for each finalized design. These preparation and evaluation steps are automated using CodeV scripts executed on the MIT Lincoln Laboratory's Integrated Modeling and Analysis Software (LLIMAS) compute system~\cite{Doyle2017LLIMAS}.

In addition to retaining each collected design in CodeV's standard sequence-file format, we represent the corpus in a tabular format for generative modeling, with one design per row. Each lens assembly comprises an ordered sequence of active surfaces defined by their radius of curvature, axial thickness, trailing material, semi-diameter, and, where applicable, conic and even-order aspheric terms. The tabular representation records these surface-level parameters together with the aperture-stop index and image-surface parameters. Collectively, these fields define the optical prescription illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:lens_param}.

\begin{figure}[ht]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\linewidth]{figures/lens_params_def_programmatic/figure_2_patent_lens_parameters.pdf}
    \caption{\textbf{Optical prescription representation.} A representative patent-derived assembly annotated with the geometric, material, aperture-stop, image-plane, and aspheric parameters retained in the tabular prescription.}

Beyond the optical prescription, each row also records the source design conditions under which the system is defined, including entrance-pupil diameter, effective focal length, and object and wavelength specifications. It also retains first-order, packaging, and structural quantities evaluated or derived from the prescription. Appendix~\ref{app:dataset_schema} lists all fields in the tabular representation together with their definitions, units, and field-indexing conventions.

The tabular representation also contains CodeV evaluation metrics: RMS spot size (SPO67), full spot size (SPO100), 50\% encircled energy (EE50), 90\% encircled energy (EE90), RMS wavefront aberration, performance sensitivity, tolerance sensitivity, and nominal alignment sensitivity. Together, the tabular fields, source attribution, and associated sequence file form the released record for each design in the collected patent-derived corpus.

\subsection{Patent Dataset Shortlisting}
\label{sec:dataset_patent_shortlisting}

The broad reference corpus intentionally preserves the heterogeneous operating conditions and optical architectures present in its source libraries. For the generative-modeling case study, we derive a more homogeneous all-spherical subset by standardizing its operating conditions and restricting its design topology. This subset also serves as the starting point for synthetic expansion. The shortlisting workflow consists of the following design filters and processing steps.

\noindent\textbf{Design filtering.} A design is retained only if it:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item has object distance specified as infinity;
    \item has air specified as material of both object and image space;
    \item uses only materials from available optical catalogs;
    \item has planar object and image surfaces;
    \item contains only spherical optical surfaces and no gratings;
    \item contains 2--8 glass elements and 3--17 active surfaces; and
    \item has glass as its final optical element, followed by air to the image plane.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textbf{Processing of retained designs.} For each retained design, the pipeline:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item standardizes prescription length units to millimeters;
    \item converts each source field definition to an angular representation defined by its maximum field angle and evaluates performance at field angles equal to $\{0,0.3,0.5,0.7,1.0\}$ times this value;
    \item represents field angles in the meridional/y-field direction, with x-field entries set to zero;
    \item standardizes spectral evaluation to the C, d, and F Fraunhofer lines with equal weights and the d line as the reference wavelength; and
    \item re-evaluates image-plane placement and dependent CodeV annotations where required.
\end{enumerate}

The design filters retain 1,253 designs and exclude 1,318 of the 2,571 collected designs. Although more constrained, the retained subset preserves substantial variation in topology and operating scale: it spans the full allowed range of 3--17 active surfaces and broad distributions of effective focal length, entrance-pupil diameter, and maximum field angle (Appendix~\ref{app:dataset_characteristics}). 

\subsection{Synthetic Expansion}
\label{sec:dataset_synthetic_expansion}

The processed and shortlisted patent dataset is expanded through controlled perturbations that preserve the same tabular prescription and CodeV sequence-file formats. This perturbation pipeline is outlined in figure~\ref{fig:synth_data_pipe}. It involves sampling few patent designs and then stochastically perturbing curvature, axial thickness, material labels or a combination of these across all the surfaces and finally post processing and filtering them to match the design conditions and remove any infeasible designs. Finally, all required metrics of generated designs are evaluated using CodeV ray-tracing evaluations.

Through this synthetic dataset generation process 1,125,080 designs are generated and evaluated from the shortlised 1253 designs. The accepted synthetic rows cover all seven non-empty combinations of curvature, thickness, and material perturbation.A detailed accounting of perturbation counts and traceability fields are reported in Appendix~\ref{app:synthetic_lineage_details}.
