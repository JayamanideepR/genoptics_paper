\begin{abstract}
Lens assembly design is a complex and time-intensive process that requires navigating a high-dimensional, variable-length design space while satisfying strict optical performance and feasibility constraints. Commercial optical design software can locally optimize well-initialized prescriptions, but identifying high-quality starting points remains a difficult and often manual task that strongly influences the final design outcome. Deep generative models have shown promise for design-space exploration in several engineering domains, but their adoption in optical system design has been limited, in part by the absence of large-scale, model-ready optical prescription datasets. In practice, candidate designs are still largely generated through expert-driven, time-consuming workflows.

To address these limitations, this paper makes five contributions toward generative optical lens design: (1) a large-scale dataset of patented and synthetically expanded lens assembly designs with associated optical performance metrics; (2) a parametric representation of optical systems designed to improve generative modeling over direct raw prescription tables; (3) a systematic benchmarking study of off-the-shelf unconditional generative models for lens prescription generation; (4) a ray-tracing-based post-processing pipeline for improving the physical feasibility, simulation readiness, and optical performance of generated samples; and (5) a conditional latent diffusion architecture for generating physically meaningful designs conditioned on design requirements.

The proposed conditional model combines a transformer-based autoencoder that embeds variable-length lens assemblies into a fixed-length latent space with a conditional diffusion model trained over the resulting latent representations. Together, the dataset, representation, benchmark suite, post-processing pipeline, and conditional architecture frame lens assembly generation as an optics-aware generative modeling problem and provide a path toward generating useful initialization points for downstream optical optimization.
\end{abstract}
